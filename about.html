Origins

Dion was born in Cyprus — or as he tells it, "practically raised in Nicosia's most legendary neighbourhood, you wouldn't know it." He moved to England sometime in the late nineties, or early 2000s, depending on which version of the story you're hearing and how many drinks have been consumed.

What's certain is that he arrived with a gift: the ability to walk into any room and, within ten minutes, have everyone convinced he's the most interesting person they've ever met. Within twenty minutes, he'll have claimed to have met someone famous. Within an hour, he'll have a drink in his hand that someone else bought him.

The Comedy

Dion discovered standup "by accident" — a claim he makes about most of his best decisions. He was at a mate's birthday, someone handed him a mic as a joke, and apparently he went for 35 minutes without stopping. Nobody left. Some people cried. A legend was, quietly, born.

His material draws from real life: the chaos of growing up Cypriot in England, the gap between Mediterranean family expectations and British pub culture, and the specific, painful comedy of being a man who genuinely believes every story he tells — even the ones he's clearly making up on the spot.

He is not a clean comedian. He is not a polished comedian. He is, however, a funny one, which in the end is the only qualification that matters.

The Barber Years

For a period that Dion refers to as "my entrepreneurial phase," he ran a barbershop. The shop no longer exists. The reasons are disputed. Dion's version involves a miscommunication with a landlord, a misunderstanding about business rates, and "honestly it was political." Former clients' versions vary — but most agree the fades were questionable and the chat was incredible.

He still cuts hair occasionally. If you ask him to, he will say yes. Our advice: don't.

The Long Game

Here's the thing about Dion. On paper — and on this website — he sounds like a nightmare. And if you've known him for twenty years, he probably is. You've heard the Ayia Napa story at least forty times. You know the punchline before he finishes the setup. You've bailed him out of a situation or two that you're not going to think about too hard right now.

But you also know this: you've never once had a bad night when Dion showed up. You've never once sat in a room with him and been bored. And at some point, despite everything, you laughed until something hurt.

That's the whole pitch, really. He's a lot. But he's worth it.

At least for one evening.

A Brief History of Dion

(As told by Dion. Dates may vary.)

1987

Born

Nicosia, Cyprus. He says there was a crowd at the hospital. He says they were waiting for him specifically. His mum says it was a Tuesday and she just wanted to go home.

Late 90s

Arrives in England

Dion relocates to the UK. Within a week he tells everyone he's been here "for years." Within a month, he has a group of mates who have absolutely no idea he's been in the country for a month.

Early 2000s

The Barber Phase

Completes what he calls "extensive training." Opens a barbershop. The shop is beloved for its atmosphere and loathed for its haircuts. It closes. He does not elaborate.

2008

The Mic Incident

Picked up a mic at a birthday party as a dare. Did 35 minutes. Nobody left. He hasn't put a mic down since — figuratively or literally.

2010–2019

"The Circuit Years"

Performs across the UK. Claims to have headlined a number of prestigious venues. Evidence is thin. The memories, he insists, are crystal clear.

Now

Available for Your Event

Wiser. Slightly more reliable. Just as loud. Still the funniest person at any given party, and he knows it, and he will tell you about it.
