Origins

Dion was born in England in 1978 — though his soul, his temper, and his attitude toward tap water are 100% Cypriot. His parents brought the Mediterranean with them: the food, the volume, the garlic, and a very particular relationship with the truth.

He arrived into the world already disappointed that no one was paying attention to him. He has spent the 47 years since correcting that. You can track how far into the evening he is by watching the index finger. When it points steadily, he's fine. When it trembles like a divining rod in a thunderstorm, you've got maybe 20 minutes before someone needs to drive him home.

The Barber

Thirty years in the trade. Thirty years. Dion can have you in and out in under six minutes — a feat that earned him the nickname Edward Scissorhands among regulars. The shaky hands, for the record, are not artistic flourish. They are the consequence of a lifestyle that begins around 9pm most nights and ends somewhere around dawn near a kebab shop.

He reeks permanently of garlic. This is deliberate — or at least, it has become deliberate. What started as a culinary preference evolved into a professional strategy: layer enough garlic onto your person and no one can smell the Henry Weston's you had at 11am. It works. Mostly.

His clients love him. The haircuts are fast, the chat is extraordinary, and you leave feeling like you've had a therapy session conducted by a man who definitely needs his own therapy session. Don't ask about the fades.

The Comedy

Dion discovered standup "by accident" — a claim he makes about most of his best decisions. Someone handed him a mic at a mate's birthday as a dare. He went for 35 minutes. Nobody left. Some people cried. A legend was, reluctantly, born.

His material is drawn from a rich and deeply specific life: Cypriot heritage colliding with English reality, 30 years of other people's haircuts, the organic food aisle at Waitrose, and an extremely complicated relationship with his own nervous system on a Tuesday night.

He will not drink tap water. He won't touch anything non-organic. He has strong opinions about what you put in your body. He also — allegedly, and he denies nothing — puts petrochemicals up his nose three to six nights a week. The body, he explains, is a temple. Some temples have more unusual rituals than others.

The Military Chapter

The photos on this site are from Dion's time in what he describes as "the Special Division of the SAS." This is technically accurate. The "Special" refers to the needs. He required an adult present at all times. He was an excellent soldier in the sense that he showed up, told everyone incredible stories, and nobody ever verified any of them.

Family Matters

Dion's mother has passed. Do not bring this up. You will not hear the end of it — partly because he grieves loudly, and partly because he claims, with a completely straight face, that he killed her. He did not kill her. She was 84 and had a full life. He says this at parties. He thinks it's funny. Half the room agrees with him.

There are other family matters we have been advised not to elaborate on. Specifically involving a sister-in-law. He denies it. We are moving on.

The Long Game

Here's the thing. On paper — and on this website — he sounds like an absolute nightmare. And if you've known him twenty years, he probably is. You've heard every story. You know the punchline. You've bailed him out of at least two situations you've agreed never to discuss again.

But here's the other thing: you've never once had a bad night when Dion showed up. He doesn't learn from his mistakes, exactly — it usually takes him 12 to 16 attempts before the lesson lands. But eventually it lands. And in the meantime, the journey is genuinely, painfully, brilliantly funny.

Book him. You won't regret it. That evening.

A Brief History of Dion

(As told by Dion. Dates may vary. Accuracy not guaranteed.)

1978

Born, England

Born in England to Cypriot parents who immediately began worrying about him. He says there was a crowd at the hospital waiting specifically for him. There was not. His mum just wanted to go home. She is no longer with us. Do not bring this up.

Mid 80s

Discovers Garlic

A formative moment. What begins as a culinary influence from his Cypriot heritage gradually becomes something more strategic. By adulthood it is a permanent, load-bearing part of his personal identity and olfactory profile.

1994

Enters the Trade

Begins his barbering career. 30 years, thousands of haircuts, and a six-minute average cut time that earns him the nickname Edward Scissorhands. The hands, it will later become clear, are not always entirely steady. The clients keep coming back anyway.

Late 90s

The Special Division

Serves in what he calls "the Special Division of the SAS." This is technically true. The photos are real. The "Special" is not what he implies. He required an adult escort at all times. He received commendations for storytelling.

Early 2000s

The Organic Awakening

Dion becomes deeply committed to organic food and filtered water. Will not touch tap water. Will not eat anything with additives. Has extremely strong views on what you put in your body. Separately, begins a 3–6 nights per week petrochemical ritual that he does not consider contradictory.

2008

The Mic Incident

Handed a mic at a birthday party as a dare. Goes for 35 minutes. Nobody leaves. A woman in the front row cries — whether from laughter or something else remains unclear. He hasn't put the mic down since.

2010–Now

The Circuit. The Legend. The Chaos.

Performs at weddings, birthdays, stag dos, corporate events, and at least one venue he definitely wasn't invited to. Makes the same mistake an average of 14 times before learning from it. Still the funniest person in any given room. Available for your event. Bring garlic bread. He will eat it.
